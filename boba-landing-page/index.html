About Boba Tea

Boba tea, also known as bubble tea, originated in Taiwan in the 1980s. The creation of this delightful drink is often credited to Lin Hsiu Hui, who added tapioca pearls to her iced tea, creating a unique and enjoyable beverage.

Originally, boba tea was a simple blend of black tea, milk, ice, and tapioca pearls. However, it has evolved over the years to include a wide variety of flavors, teas, and toppings. The term "bubble tea" comes from the frothy bubbles created when the tea is shaken or blended.

Today, boba tea is enjoyed worldwide, with countless variations and combinations. It has become a popular and trendy drink, loved for its refreshing taste and the fun experience of sipping through a wide straw to enjoy the chewy tapioca pearls.
